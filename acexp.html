Research

Low Power Positioning System

Under Dr.Gugel I developed a low power system that can determine when it is at the top of a spinning mill. Using an MSP430 and accelerometer, 3-axis acceleration data was read and filtered to calculate its position and velocity. The entire system ran on 2 microamperes.

Through this experience I learned good coding practice for low power embedded systems.

Approximate Computing

In Spring 2016 under Dr.Stitt, I will be researching Approximate Computing. The goal is to develop digital architectures that can approximate close answers, sacrificing accuracy for increased processing speed and parallelization.
